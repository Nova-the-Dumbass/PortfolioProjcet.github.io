Hobbies and Interests

Outside of work, I have a few hobbies that I look forward to when I get home. Though none of them specifically relate to computer science or much of computing at all, I do believe they can highlight my strengths, as they can show the fact that I may be able to think on my feet, or plan strategically for the future, or that I have patience when it comes to a task that involves a lot of trial and error. Admittedly, I also just find them fun to share and talk about.
Without further ado, these are my main hobbies and what I am interested in most outside of working with computers and technology.

Speedcubing

Speedcubing is one of my favorite hobbies that I've undertaken. You may be somwehat familiar with the Rubik's Cube, and how the objective is to solve it by putting one color on each face of the cube. You may also be familiar with how imfamously difficult the puzzle is for many people and how it's often considered a thing for genuises. Truth be told, that isn't the case; most people can learn to solve one if given enough time and they dedicate enough practice.

Though a handful of people like to solve the cube casually, there is a large group of people, including myself who enjoy trying to solve the cube as fast as possible.

There are many other Rubik's Cube inspired puzzles that exist that people like to compete for the fastest times, such as the Megaminx or the Square-1.
I personally gravitate towards the classic 3x3, 4x4, and 5x5 Rubik's cubes due to how fun they are to solve in my opinion, however I do enjoy other puzzles such as the Pyraminx or Square-1 from time to time, or even higher orders of the standard Rubik's cube, such as the 7x7 cube.

My fastest times on the 3x3, 4x4, and 5x5 are 7.78 seconds, 50.31 seconds, and 2:01.47 respectively. I practice solving the 3x3 the most by a large margin, which is why my personal best for it is relatively lower than that of the 4x4 and 5x5.

Why am I sharing this?

I believe that you can learn a lot from somebody just from what they like to do in their spare time. They can give you a glimpse into their strengths and show what that person is interested in. For example, I enjoy hands-on experiences and enjoy strategizing and thinking on my feet, as can be seen with my love of speedcubing. You always need to plan ahead and think quickly to achieve a good time. Or, as seen in my passion for drawing, I enjoy creating and designing new things, and it shows the fact that I can think ahead and plan for a long-term project.
